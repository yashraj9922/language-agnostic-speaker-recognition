────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  OVERVIEW
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  This document traces a single audio clip through every mathematical operation
  from raw waveform → 256-dim L2-normalized speaker embedding, explaining
  exactly where matrix multiplications happen, which weights are frozen vs.
  fine-tuned, and how those updated numbers change the final output.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  STEP 0 — INPUT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Raw waveform:
    x ∈ ℝ^32000     (2 seconds × 16,000 Hz = 32,000 samples)
    Each element is a floating-point amplitude value between -1 and 1.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  STEP 1 — CNN FEATURE EXTRACTOR  [❄️ Frozen]
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  7 convolutional layers compress the raw waveform into frames.

  Operation per layer:
    h[t] = Σ  W_conv[k] · x[t·stride + k]     for each filter k
           k

  Total stride = 320 across all 7 layers.
  At 16,000 Hz:  32,000 / 320 = 100 frames

  Output:
    H ∈ ℝ^(100 × 512)     (100 frames, 512-dim each, one frame = 20 ms)

  Status:  FROZEN — W_conv never changed during fine-tuning.
  Purpose: Extracts raw acoustic features — energy, pitch contours, formants.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  STEP 2 — LINEAR PROJECTION INTO TRANSFORMER SPACE  [❄️ Frozen]
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Lifts each frame from 512-dim to 1024-dim (Transformer hidden size).

  Operation:
    H_proj = H · W_proj  +  b_proj

    H       ∈ ℝ^(100 × 512)
    W_proj  ∈ ℝ^(512 × 1024)
    ──────────────────────────
    Result  ∈ ℝ^(100 × 1024)

  Status:  FROZEN


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  STEP 3 — TRANSFORMER LAYERS 1–20  [❄️ Frozen]
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Each of the 20 layers performs the same 3 operations.
  Tracking one layer (input H ∈ ℝ^(100 × 1024)):

  ── 3A. Self-Attention ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    Q = H · W_Q          (Query)        W_Q ∈ ℝ^(1024 × 1024)
    K = H · W_K          (Key)          W_K ∈ ℝ^(1024 × 1024)
    V = H · W_V          (Value)        W_V ∈ ℝ^(1024 × 1024)

    Attention scores:
      A = softmax( Q · Kᵀ / √64 )      A ∈ ℝ^(100 × 100)
                         ↑
                   √(head_dim) = √(1024 / 16 heads) = √64

    Attended output:
      Z = A · V                         Z ∈ ℝ^(100 × 1024)

    Meaning: Every frame "looks at" all other frames.
             Frame 50 can borrow context from frame 10.

  ── 3B. Feed-Forward Network ────────────────────────────────────────────────

    FFN(Z) = ReLU( Z · W_1 + b_1 ) · W_2 + b_2

    W_1 ∈ ℝ^(1024 × 4096)    ← expand
    W_2 ∈ ℝ^(4096 × 1024)    ← compress back

  ── 3C. Residual + LayerNorm ────────────────────────────────────────────────

    H_out = LayerNorm( H + Z )
    H_out = LayerNorm( H_out + FFN(H_out) )

  Status:  FROZEN — W_Q, W_K, W_V, W_1, W_2 are original pretrained values.
  Purpose: These layers encode phonetic and acoustic structure of speech.
           They know what sounds like what — not who sounds like who.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  STEP 4 — TRANSFORMER LAYERS 21–24  [🔥 Fine-tuned ← KEY STEP]
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  SAME mathematical operations as Step 3 — but the weight matrices
  contain DIFFERENT numbers from what pretraining left them at.

  Before fine-tuning, layer 21's W_Q encoded:
    "How much does frame i's phoneme context
     relate to frame j's phoneme context?"

  After fine-tuning, layer 21's W_Q encodes:
    "How much does frame i's SPEAKER characteristic
     relate to frame j's SPEAKER characteristic?"

  How the weights changed — gradient descent:
    W_new = W_old  -  lr · ∂Loss/∂W

    ∂Loss/∂W flows backward from AAM-Softmax
    → through Projection Head
    → through Attentive Pool
    → through Layers 24 → 23 → 22 → 21

  Over 7,500 gradient steps (750 batches × 10 epochs), each tiny update
  nudged W_Q, W_K, W_V, W_1, W_2 in these 4 layers to make their
  attention patterns SPEAKER-SELECTIVE:

    Vowel frames (carry speaker identity)   → high attention scores
    Silence / noise frames                  → near-zero attention scores
    Consonant frames                        → moderate attention scores

  Status:  FINE-TUNED — weights shifted from phoneme-focus to speaker-focus.
  Output:  H ∈ ℝ^(100 × 1024)   (same shape, different content)


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  STEP 5 — ATTENTIVE STATISTICS POOLING  [🔥 Fine-tuned]
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Input:  H ∈ ℝ^(100 × 1024)    — 100 frames, each 1024-dim

  ── Attention Scores ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    e = H  · W_a1              W_a1 ∈ ℝ^(1024 × 128)    e ∈ ℝ^(100 × 128)
    e = tanh(e)
    e = e  · W_a2              W_a2 ∈ ℝ^(128 × 1)       e ∈ ℝ^(100 × 1)
    α = softmax(e)                                        α ∈ ℝ^(100 × 1)
        ↑
    α[t] = how important is frame t for speaker identity

  ── Weighted Mean ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    μ = αᵀ · H  =  Σ α[t] · H[t]    for t = 1..100       μ ∈ ℝ^(1 × 1024)

  ── Weighted Standard Deviation ─────────────────────────────────────────────

    σ² = Σ α[t] · ( H[t] - μ )²
    σ  = sqrt(σ²)                                          σ ∈ ℝ^(1 × 1024)

  ── Concatenate ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    out = [ μ | σ ]                                        out ∈ ℝ^(1 × 2048)

  Concrete α values for a typical 2-second clip:
    Silence frames    →  α ≈ 0.002
    Vowel frames      →  α ≈ 0.025
    Consonant frames  →  α ≈ 0.010

  Status:  FINE-TUNED — W_a1, W_a2 learned to weight speaker-rich frames.
  Purpose: Collapses variable-length sequence into fixed-size summary.
           μ captures the speaker's central tendency on the hypersphere.
           σ captures speaking style variation across frames.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  STEP 6 — PROJECTION HEAD  [🔥 Fine-tuned]
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Compresses 2048-dim pooled representation into final 256-dim embedding space.

  ── Layer 1 ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    z1 = out · W_p1  +  b_p1       W_p1 ∈ ℝ^(2048 × 512)
    z1 = BatchNorm(z1)              normalizes scale across the batch
    z1 = ReLU(z1)                   kills negatives, preserves structure
    z1 ∈ ℝ^512

  ── Layer 2 ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    z2 = z1 · W_p2  +  b_p2       W_p2 ∈ ℝ^(512 × 256)
    z2 ∈ ℝ^256

  Status:  FINE-TUNED — W_p1, W_p2 learned to arrange the 256-dim space
           such that intra-speaker distance is minimized and
           inter-speaker distance is maximized.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  STEP 7 — L2 NORMALIZATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

    ||z2||₂ = sqrt( z2[0]² + z2[1]² + ... + z2[255]² )

    v = z2 / ||z2||₂

    Result:  ||v|| = 1   always
             v ∈ ℝ^256,  lives exactly on the surface of a unit hypersphere

  Why this matters:
    Without normalization, vector magnitude also carries information (noise).
    After normalization, ONLY DIRECTION matters.
    AAM-Softmax uses cos(θ) everywhere — it is designed for the unit sphere.
    L2 norm is not an afterthought; training directly optimizes direction.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  STEP 8 — COSINE SIMILARITY AT VERIFICATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  For two audio clips A and B:
    emb_A = v_A ∈ ℝ^256,   ||v_A|| = 1
    emb_B = v_B ∈ ℝ^256,   ||v_B|| = 1

    score = v_A · v_Bᵀ  =  Σ v_A[i] · v_B[i]     for i = 0..255
                            ↑
              dot product = cosine similarity  (since both are unit vectors)

    score ∈ [-1.0, 1.0]

    Same speaker      →  embeddings point same direction  →  score ≈ 0.71
    Different speaker →  embeddings point apart           →  score ≈ 0.37

    Decision:
      score ≥ threshold (~0.54)  →  ✅  Same speaker
      score <  threshold         →  ❌  Different speaker


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  FULL MATHEMATICAL PIPELINE — SUMMARY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  x ∈ ℝ^32000
    │
    │  W_conv            ← FROZEN    extracts acoustic features
    ↓
  H ∈ ℝ^(100 × 512)
    │
    │  W_proj            ← FROZEN    lifts to transformer space
    ↓
  H ∈ ℝ^(100 × 1024)
    │
    │  W_Q, W_K, W_V     ← FROZEN    phoneme-focused attention
    │  W_1, W_2          ← FROZEN    (layers 1–20, unchanged from pretraining)
    ↓
  H ∈ ℝ^(100 × 1024)
    │
    │  W_Q, W_K, W_V     ← FINE-TUNED   speaker-focused attention
    │  W_1, W_2          ← FINE-TUNED   (layers 21–24, shifted by 7,500 steps)
    ↓
  H ∈ ℝ^(100 × 1024)
    │
    │  W_a1, W_a2        ← FINE-TUNED   frame importance weights
    ↓
  [μ | σ] ∈ ℝ^2048
    │
    │  W_p1, W_p2        ← FINE-TUNED   compress to embedding space
    ↓
  z ∈ ℝ^256
    │
    │  / ||z||₂          ← project onto unit sphere
    ↓
  v ∈ ℝ^256,  ||v|| = 1                 ←  FINAL SPEAKER EMBEDDING

  dot(v_A, v_B)  →  similarity score  →  threshold  →  Same / Different


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  WHAT FINE-TUNING CHANGED — CONCRETELY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  The matrix multiplication formula  H_out = A · V  is IDENTICAL before and
  after fine-tuning. What changed is the NUMBERS INSIDE the weight matrices.

                          Before Fine-tuning    After Fine-tuning (ep 9)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Same speaker score        ~0.40 – 0.60          ~0.71
  Different speaker score   ~0.30 – 0.50          ~0.37
  Score separation (Δμ)     ~0.10                 ~0.34
  Val EER                   ~45%                   8.25%
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Fine-tuning widened the genuine/impostor gap from ~0.10 to ~0.34.
  That gap is what makes thresholding meaningful and verification possible.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  KEY INSIGHT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Every matrix multiplication  (H · W)  is the same mathematical operation
  before and after fine-tuning. What changes is the numbers inside W.

  Fine-tuning = iteratively adjusting those numbers over 7,500 gradient steps
  so that when speaker audio passes through the entire chain, the final
  256-dim direction on the unit sphere encodes IDENTITY, not CONTENT.
